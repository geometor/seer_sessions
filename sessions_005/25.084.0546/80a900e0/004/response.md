**Natural Language Program:**

1.  **Checkerboard Identification:** The input grid contains a checkerboard pattern defined by alternating cells of white (0) and blue (1).
2.  **Source Pixel Identification:** Identify "source pixels" as those cells that are:
    *   White (value 0)
    *   Contain a color other than white (0) or blue (1). These are considered the "initial colors".
3.  **Propagation Rule:** For each source pixel:
    *   Copy its color to other cells within the grid.
    *   The target cells for propagation are determined by the checkerboard pattern. A target cell must maintain the same "parity" of row and column indices as the source pixel. This means that if the source cell is at an (even, even) row and column, the target cell must also be at an (even, even) row and column. The same logic applies for (even, odd), (odd, even), and (odd, odd) combinations.
    * The propagation extends outwards in all four diagonal directions.

In simpler terms: Find the colored pixels on the white squares of the checkerboard. Copy those colors to all other white squares that are reachable by moving diagonally any number of "hops" of size 2.
